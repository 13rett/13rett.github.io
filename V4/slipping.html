Slipping

     Kinetic Typographies are a popular on Youtube and have also been seen as commercials on television. They are often targeting people who are already fans and teenagers. In this project each student was assigned to use a 30 second clip of a song and create a kinetic typography video. Using purely text we had to capture the meaning and feel of the lyrics. I used “Slipping” from Dr. Horrible’s Sing Along Blog. The biggest issue was  to set up the text in a way that allowed me to animate it how I wanted to. As I was unfamiliar with Adobe Premiere I had not set up the text correctly for the animations I wanted it to perform. Most of the times I had set up the text wrong it was simply a matter of regrouping the text or editing a section. A couple of times however I was unsure the setup needed to achieve the animation and I altered the animation to work with what I knew.

 

Skills Used: Typography, syncing visuals to audio, video editing, keyframe animation, creative analysis and creative visual representation.
